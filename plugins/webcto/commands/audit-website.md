Run a full WebCTO audit for the following target. Treat this command as the all-in-one plugin entry point.

Target:

$ARGUMENTS

Use all relevant WebCTO skills:

- `cto-website-intelligence`
- `business-truth-checker`
- `mvp-scope-reducer`
- `product-roadmap`
- `crawler-architecture`
- `seo-audit`
- `performance-audit`
- `accessibility-audit`
- `security-audit`
- `conversion-audit`
- `report-generator`
- `technical-acceptance-criteria`

If the target is a public URL, inspect the live site when browsing/fetch tools are available. Try to inspect the homepage, robots.txt, sitemap.xml, and up to three important inner pages. If live browsing, scanners, headers, Lighthouse, or source access are unavailable, keep going with a partial audit and mark those sections as unverified. Do not stop after one category.

Required output:

1. Overall readiness score
2. Evidence summary: what was directly checked, inferred, or unavailable
3. Business truth check
4. MVP scope recommendation
5. Product roadmap
6. Crawler and platform architecture review
7. SEO audit
8. Performance audit
9. Accessibility audit
10. Security audit
11. Conversion audit
12. Reporting/dashboard recommendations
13. Technical acceptance criteria
14. Top 10 prioritized fixes
15. Next three actions

For every section include:

- score or status
- findings
- evidence or limitation
- recommended fix
- verification step

Use concise tables where useful. Be direct, but do not invent facts that were not observed or provided.
